Geographic Vectors

Hypothesis

There are no counties in the United States without a cellphone tower within it's boundaries.

Data Sources

All data was taken from https://data.world/dhs/cellular-towers

Results

My hypothesis stemmed from the understanding that cell towers become more scarce the further west you are in the United States while counties also tend to become larger, allowing for a higher chance of containing at least one cell phone tower. After visualizing the data I found that there are in fact multiple US counties without a cellphone tower within their boundaries. However, it is a very small amount. A measly 10 out of 3,006 counties in the US do not have a cell phone tower within their boundaries as of 2016. It is possible that in 2022 these counties have since aquired a tower but the only applicable data I could find was from 2016.

The most interesting part of this visualization is the northmost void in Texas. This is where Dallas is located. This makes me think that there is some sort of law or policy that does not allow cell phone towers to be built in the metropolitan area of Dallas.

Color Choice

When choosing a color I noticed that desaturated colors made the cell tower voids subside whereas a very bright and saturated blue made them stand out to the eye. Being that these voids are the focus of this visualization I wanted them to stand out but was also aware that a very saturated blue made the visualization feel unprofessional. I found that darkening the blue rather than desaturating it achieved this without sacrificing the professional credibility of the visualization.
